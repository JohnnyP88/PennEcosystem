Overview

In today’s rapidly changing markets, key variables — from supply chain and technology to product development and strategic partnerships — add multiple layers of complexity to the already volatile landscape. This requires mature organizations to chart a path for the future by reconsidering the effects these variables can have on their organization while also assessing their capabilities for innovation and developing road maps for strategies that maintain their competitive edge. They must also recognize the importance of creating strategic alliances that can enhance their innovation efforts and overall success. This program is designed to help business leaders and executives learn to build such an innovative ecosystem, one that’s focused on the strategic objectives of their organization and leveraging technological partnerships. As a participant, you will learn best practices for managing both strategy and innovation through real-world examples drawn from organizations such as Tesla, Boeing, Walmart, and more.

Key Takeaways

Wharton’s Driving Strategic Innovation: Leading Complex Initiatives for Impact program is designed to prepare business leaders for creating and executing a well-developed innovation strategy that ensures their organization can meet the challenges of today and the future. Throughout the program, you will:

  • Discover strategies to drive innovation within your organization
  • Learn to balance exploration and exploitation as part of an organizational strategy
  • Evaluate commercializing technology innovation through partnerships
  • Gain an understanding of innovation ecosystems, and learn how to successfully manage them
  • Explore open-innovation models and applications
  • Examine large-scale innovation implementation and management within organizations

Who Is This Program For?

The program is designed for business leaders and professionals across industries seeking to:

  • Understand the commercialization of technology solutions
  • Learn how to drive successful strategic initiatives in their department or organization
  • Expand their knowledge of large-scale innovation and implementation and management
  • Learn about the ecosystem approach to innovation in business

Harbir Singh, PhD

Mack Professor; Professor of Management; Co-Director; Mack Institute for Innovation Management; Faculty Director; Huntsman Program

Harbir Singh is the Mack Professor of Management; co-director, Mack Institute for Innovation Management; and faculty director of the Huntsman Program for International Studies and Business at the Wharton School. Committed to global business research and education, he has served as vice dean for global initiatives at the Wharton School. He focuses on the development of world-class capabilities and of sustainable competitive advantage. His main interests in research and teaching lie in three areas: strategic leadership, growth and innovation via alliances and acquisitions, and the evolution of competitive strategy.

Professor Singh has won multiple awards for outstanding research in strategic management at the Academy of Management in addition to awards for teaching. He received the Academy of International Business Decade Award for his co-authored work on determinants of modes of entry by multinational firms into new country markets. He was chair of the Business Policy and Strategy Division of the Academy of Management and is the former dean of the fellows of the Strategic Management Society. He is the author of several books on strategy and leadership in a global setting.

Professor Singh received his PhD at the University of Michigan. He is the academic director for several of Wharton’s open-enrollment executive programs, including Creating and Implementing Strategy for Competitive Advantage and Driving Growth through Strategic Partnerships, and is a lead faculty member in the Mergers and Acquisitions program. Additionally, he teaches and directs numerous customized programs for companies and associations around the world.

Professor Singh has been a visiting faculty member at London Business School; Bocconi University in Milan; and the University of St. Gallen, Switzerland, as well as a founding faculty member of the Indian School of Business. He has also conducted executive seminars on competitive advantage, globalization, acquisitions, and alliances for many companies including IBM Corporation, Pfizer, Verizon, AT&T, and Daimler.
